**Q1. How did Train Kids start? Is there a founding story?**

_Train Kids was founded by two Early Interventionists who began their careers working in a Voluntary Welfare Organisation (VWO) early intervention centre. Through their years on the ground, they saw firsthand how much families needed — and how much more could be done with the right structure in place._

_What drove them to set up Train Kids was a simple but powerful belief: children with developmental needs deserve more time to learn, not less. Many existing programmes offered limited session hours, leaving little room for meaningful progress or for parents to catch their breath. Train Kids was built to change that._

_From the start, the centre offered longer sessions of up to four hours — giving each child the time and space to truly engage with learning, and giving parents the much-needed respite to attend to their own wellbeing. Because when parents are supported, children thrive too._

_Today, that founding philosophy remains at the heart of everything Train Kids does: unhurried, individualised intervention that meets each child where they are — and walks with them, every step of the way._

**Q2. What is your mission or philosophy in one sentence?**

_To provide every child with a solid foundation in learning and independent skills — because every child deserves the right to education and early intervention._

**Q3. Who are your therapists? (job titles, qualifications, experience)**

_Our multidisciplinary team includes qualified Early Interventionists, Occupational Therapists, and Speech Therapists, each experienced in working with children with special needs. All staff are trained in evidence-based approaches and standardised assessment tools (including AEPS)._

**Q4. Are you registered with or recognised by any government bodies?**

**Q2. For each programme: who is it for, and what does a typical session look like?**

**Early Intervention Programme (EIPIC)**

_For children aged 24 months to 6 years old with developmental delays or disabilities. Sessions combine one-to-one time with the child's Early Interventionist and small group activities. Goals are set using standardised assessments (AEPS) and captured in an Individualised Education Programme (IEP). Each session targets developmental domains such as communication, cognition, motor skills, and daily living skills. Caregiver involvement is embedded throughout — parents receive strategies to continue learning at home._

**Occupational Therapy**

_For children with sensory processing difficulties, fine motor delays, or challenges with daily living tasks. OT sessions help children become as independent as possible in areas such as self-care, play, and school readiness. Sessions may include sensory activities, hand-strengthening tasks, and functional skill practice._

**Speech Therapy**

_For children with speech delays, language difficulties, or communication challenges. Sessions are designed to be engaging and low-stress, using play-based techniques. A customised therapy plan is developed for each child and reviewed regularly._

**Before & After School Care (SPED Students)**

_For children aged 7–12 (Woodlands) or 7–14 (Tampines) who are currently enrolled in SPED schools. Activities cover social skills, communication, cognitive development, fine motor skills, and adaptive daily living — supporting both the child's growth and working parents' schedules._

**Q3. Are sessions individual, group, or both?**

_Both. Early Intervention sessions include individual one-to-one time as well as small group activities, allowing children to practise skills in a supported social setting._

**Q4. What age range do you serve overall?**

_2 to 14 years old, depending on the programme. Early Intervention is for children aged 2–6; Before/After School Care is for school-going children aged 7–14._
