## 1. Executive Summary

Train Kids Early Intervention & Educare Centre is launching a new brochure website designed to convert parent prospects into WhatsApp enquiries and, ultimately, centre enrolments. The website serves as the primary digital funnel for parents searching for early intervention services for children aged 2–14 with developmental delays and disabilities in Singapore.

**Core Principle**: Every element guides parents toward one primary action—texting us on WhatsApp—while building trust through clear messaging, social proof, and expert positioning.

**Success Definition**: Maximize lead-to-enrolment conversion rate (visitors → WhatsApp enquiries → paid enrolments).

## 3. Target Users & Personas

### Primary User Persona: **"Concerned Parent"**

**Demographics:**
- Age: 28–45
- Location: Singapore (Woodlands, Tampines, or nearby neighborhoods)
- Household Income: $3,000–$8,000+ (able to afford therapy services; may rely on government subsidies)
- Education: Tertiary or higher

**Goals:**
- Find evidence-based, qualified support for their child's developmental delay or diagnosis (Autism, ADHD, Speech Delay, etc.)
- Understand what early intervention can achieve and how quickly
- Get reassurance that their child is in capable, warm hands
- Learn practical strategies to support their child at home
- Access government subsidies or understand costs upfront

**Pain Points:**
- Uncertainty about whether their child "needs help" or if it's just normal development
- Overwhelmed by options and unsure how to evaluate quality
- Concern about cost and affordability
- Limited time (working parent, caring for child)
- Anxiety about their child's future and educational transition

**Behavior:**
- Googles "speech therapy Singapore," "early intervention," "child developmental delay"
- Visits 3–5 centres before deciding
- Wants to see qualified staff, real student outcomes, and parent testimonials
- Prefers WhatsApp/text for initial contact (more private, asynchronous)
- Reads reviews and looks for ECDA certification and government recognition

## 5. Design & Brand Guidelines

### Brand Identity Application

#### **Color Palette** (from Train Kids logo)
- **Primary Colors**: 
  - Red (#FF0000 or logo red)
  - Green (#00AA00 or logo green)
  - Yellow (#FFFF00 or logo yellow)
  - Blue (#0000FF or logo blue)
  - Orange (#FF8800 or logo orange)

- **Neutral Colors**:
  - White (#FFFFFF) – background
  - Dark Gray (#333333) – body text
  - Light Gray (#F5F5F5) – section backgrounds, dividers

#### **Logo Usage**
- Place Train Kids logo prominently in header (left-aligned, ~40–60px height on desktop, ~30px on mobile)
- Maintain brand tagline visibility: "We Teach. We Guide. We Encourage."
- No logo modifications; use as-is from brand files

#### **Typography**
- **Headline Font**: Clear, friendly, modern sans-serif (e.g., Poppins, Montserrat, or system sans-serif)
  - H1: 48px–56px (desktop), 32px (mobile), bold, dark gray (#333)
  - H2: 32px–40px (desktop), 24px (mobile), bold, dark gray
  - H3: 24px–28px (desktop), 18px (mobile), semi-bold, dark gray

- **Body Text**: Readable sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Roboto, system sans-serif)
  - 16px–18px (desktop), 14px–16px (mobile), line-height 1.6, dark gray
  - All text must meet WCAG AA contrast standards (4.5:1 minimum for body text)

- **CTA Button Text**: 16px, bold, white text on colored background

#### **Visual Style**
- **Tone**: Warm, hopeful, professional, child-friendly (but not childish)
- **Imagery**: 
  - Warm interactions between children and therapists
  - Diverse representation (children of different ethnicities, abilities, family structures)
  - Playful but professional (no clip art; use real photos or high-quality illustrations)
  - If using illustrations: rounded, friendly shapes; use brand colors
- **Icons**: Use brand colors; simple, friendly icon style for program cards, trust badges, etc.
- **Spacing**: Generous whitespace; sections separated by 60–80px vertical padding

#### **Color Application by Section**
- **Hero Section**: White background, brand colors in text/buttons, large imagery
- **Trust Badges**: Use all 5 brand colors in a circular/grid pattern (red badge, green badge, etc.)
- **Programme Cards**: Each program gets a dominant brand color (EIP = Red, OT = Green, Speech = Yellow, SPED Care = Blue, etc.)
- **CTAs/Buttons**: Primary CTA (WhatsApp) = Green or Blue (high contrast, trustworthy)
- **Milestones/Stories**: Alternating background colors (light gray, light red, light green, etc.) for visual rhythm

#### **Responsive Design**
- **Mobile-First Approach**: Design for 375px width first, then scale up
- **Breakpoints**: 
  - Mobile: 320px–767px
  - Tablet: 768px–1023px
  - Desktop: 1024px+
- **Mobile Navigation**: 
  - Hamburger menu (3-line icon) in top-right
  - Sticky WhatsApp button (floating action button) at bottom-right on mobile
- **Sticky Header**: Navigation bar remains visible when scrolling; logo + menu + WhatsApp CTA always accessible

## 7. Success Metrics & KPIs

### Primary KPI: **Lead-to-Enrolment Conversion Rate**

**Definition**: Percentage of website visitors who message on WhatsApp → Percentage of WhatsApp leads who enrol in a programme

**Target Metrics** (baseline, to be refined post-launch):
- **Website Traffic**: 100–200 visitors/month (after SEO & organic growth)
- **WhatsApp Click-Through Rate**: 5–10% of visitors click WhatsApp CTA
- **WhatsApp Enquiries**: 5–15 per month (from website traffic)
- **Enquiry-to-Visit Conversion**: 60–70% of enquiries schedule a centre visit
- **Visit-to-Enrolment Conversion**: 40–60% of centre visits result in enrolment
- **Overall Funnel Conversion**: 1–3% of website visitors → enrolled students

### Secondary KPIs
- **Page Load Time**: <3 seconds (desktop), <4 seconds (mobile)
- **Mobile Usability**: 90+ score in Google PageSpeed Insights
- **Bounce Rate**: <50% (engagement metric)
- **Average Time on Site**: >2 minutes
- **Therapy Tips Page Traffic**: 20–30% of total site visits (engagement/content value)
- **Social Media Referrals**: Track Facebook visits via UTM parameters
- **Return Visits**: 30–40% of visitors return within 30 days

### Measurement & Analytics
- **Platform**: Google Analytics 4 (GA4)
- **Events to Track**:
  - WhatsApp button click (all pages)
  - CTA clicks (e.g., "Learn More" → Programme page)
  - Form submissions (if contact form added later)
  - Page views (all 6 pages)
  - External link clicks (Facebook, WhatsApp)
- **Goals to Set Up**:
  - Goal 1: WhatsApp CTA click
  - Goal 2: Visit Contact page
  - Goal 3: Return visits
- **Reporting**: Monthly dashboard review; adjust messaging/design based on drop-off patterns

**Platform**: Static website (HTML/CSS/JavaScript) or headless CMS (e.g., Webflow, Framer, Next.js)

1. **Option A (Fastest MVP)**: Webflow or Framer

## 10. Accessibility & Performance Standards

### Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 Level AA)

**Minimum Requirements:**
- ✓ Contrast Ratio: Text must have 4.5:1 contrast ratio (normal), 3:1 (large text)
- ✓ Keyboard Navigation: All interactive elements accessible via Tab key
- ✓ Alt Text: All images have descriptive alt text (not "image.jpg")
- ✓ Form Labels: All inputs have associated labels (screen reader readable)
- ✓ Headings: Proper heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3, no skips)
- ✓ Color Alone**: Don't use color as the only way to convey information (use text + icons)
- ✓ Focus Indicators: Visible focus state on buttons and links (especially for mobile users)
- ✓ Page Language: HTML `lang` attribute set to "en" (English)

**Testing Tools:**
- WAVE (WebAIM) – free browser extension
- Axe DevTools – free accessibility audit
- Google Lighthouse – built-in accessibility scoring
- Manual testing with keyboard-only navigation

### Performance Standards

**Core Web Vitals Targets** (Google's ranking factors):
- **Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)**: <2.5 seconds (when largest element is visible)
- **First Input Delay (FID)**: <100 milliseconds (responsiveness to user input)
- **Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)**: <0.1 (visual stability, no unexpected layout jumps)

**Image Optimization:**
- All images compressed (PNG: 40–60% reduction, JPEG: 60–80% reduction)
- Responsive images (different sizes for mobile/desktop)
- Lazy loading for below-the-fold images
- WebP format support with PNG fallback

**Code Optimization:**
- Minify CSS, JavaScript, HTML
- Remove unused CSS (tree-shaking)
- Code-split JavaScript (load only what's needed per page)
- Limit third-party scripts (GA4, Facebook pixel)
